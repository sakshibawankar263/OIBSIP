About Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He was an aerospace scientist, educator, and the 11th President of India.

Known as the Missile Man of India, he played a major role in India's missile and nuclear programs. His dedication to science, education, and nation-building inspired millions of students across the country.

Throughout his life, he encouraged young people to dream big, work hard, and contribute positively to society.

Major Achievements

ISRO Contributions

Played a key role in India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite.

DRDO Leadership

Led several missile development projects and strengthened India's defense technology capabilities.

Pokhran-II Tests

Contributed significantly to India's successful nuclear tests conducted in 1998.

Bharat Ratna

Received India's highest civilian award for his exceptional contribution to science and society.

President of India

Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007 and became widely known as the People's President.
