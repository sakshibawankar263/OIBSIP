About Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He was an aerospace scientist, educator, and the 11th President of India.

Known as the Missile Man of India, he played a major role in India's missile and nuclear programs. His dedication to science, education, and nation-building inspired millions of students across the country.

Throughout his life, he encouraged young people to dream big, work hard, and contribute positively to society.

Major Achievements

ISRO Contributions

Played a key role in India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite.

DRDO Leadership

Led several missile development projects and strengthened India's defense technology capabilities.

Pokhran-II Tests

Contributed significantly to India's successful nuclear tests conducted in 1998.

Bharat Ratna

Received India's highest civilian award for his exceptional contribution to science and society.

President of India

Served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007 and became widely known as the People's President.

Journey Through Time

1931
Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Coming from a humble family, he developed a strong work ethic and curiosity that shaped his future journey.
1960
After completing Aerospace Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology, Kalam began his career as a scientist. This marked the beginning of his contribution to India's technological growth.
1969
Kalam joined ISRO and became part of India's ambitious space program. His work helped lay the foundation for indigenous satellite launch technology.
1998
He played a crucial role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests. The success of these tests strengthened India's strategic and scientific standing on the global stage.
2002
Kalam became the 11th President of India. His simplicity, vision, and connection with students made him one of the most respected Presidents in Indian history.
2015
Dr. Kalam passed away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong. Even in his final moments, he was doing what he loved most: educating and inspiring students.
